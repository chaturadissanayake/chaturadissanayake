DATA VISUALISATIONS

A selection of my recent data visualisation and design work.

Sri Lanka Government Budget

This Sankey diagram provides a comprehensive and intuitive overview of the Sri Lankan government's fiscal architecture. It meticulously traces the flow of public funds, beginning with primary revenue streams such as tax collections and non-tax revenue. The visualization then clearly delineates how these funds are allocated across various expenditure categories, including recurrent costs like public sector salaries and welfare, alongside capital investments in infrastructure and development projects. The diagram's key strength lies in its ability to instantly highlight the national deficit—the significant gap between incoming revenue and outgoing expenditure—and pinpoint exactly how this deficit is financed, whether through domestic borrowing, foreign loans, or other financial mechanisms. This granular, end-to-end view transforms complex budgetary tables into a clear, accessible narrative, making it an invaluable tool for policymakers, economists, and citizens.
