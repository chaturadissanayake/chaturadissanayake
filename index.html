How I work

I'm a freelance communications specialist working in the data visualisation field. What that means in practice is that I spend a lot of time thinking about relationships — between organisations and the people they're trying to reach, between data and the person who needs to act on it.

Good communication isn't about making things look nice. It's about making sure the right message lands with the right person. Sometimes that's a policy report for government partners. Sometimes it's an interactive story for a general audience who've never read a policy brief in their life. The work changes; the question stays the same: who needs to understand this, and what do they actually need to see?

I've worked with Verité Research, the UN Resident Coordinator's Office, and the Department of National Planning. Now I work independently, taking on projects where I think I can make a real difference to how the work reaches people.
